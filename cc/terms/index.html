Ethics

Coaching Canines adheres to the Association of Pet Behaviour Counsellors Code of Conduct which means I use scientifically based, modern training methods. Harsh handling is discouraged, with positive reinforcement encouraged instead. All training between trainer and client is confidential. Coaching Canines does not allow the use of any piece of equipment that can cause pain and discomfort for a dog. This includes slip leads, choke chains, pinch collars, shock collars, citronella collars, rattle cans, spray bottles, compressed air canisters, or any other device or method, that could cause harm, pain, suffering or fear for a dog. Any clients who continue to use harsh handling methods will have any sessions cancelled, with no refund given. Coaching Canines reserves the right not to work with any client if the trainer does not wish to, and no reason has to be given. Working with clients is at the discretion of Coaching Canines.

Coaching Canines reserve the right to change the timing, date or location of a training sessions in order to best support training, or where reasons beyond our control make it necessary. Sessions further than 15 miles from Coaching Canines address will incur a fee of 30p per mile, including the return journey. No refund of the deposit (50% of the total amount) will be given if any session is cancelled, but, at the discretion of Coaching Canines, sessions may be re-scheduled with at least 48 hours notice.

Health and Wellbeing

Your dog's health and wellbeing is paramount to Coaching Canines. Any dogs who are unable to partake in training sessions due to injury or illness should not be involved in any training. Any dogs with infectious disease, such as kennel cough, should not be placed into an environment where this is able to spread to other dogs. If you are unsure of the cause of your dogs symptoms, a vet must be consulted. Dogs should be vaccinated or have a titre test as evidence of immunity. It is advised that puppies who haven't finished their vaccination course should not walk on the floor outside the training venue, and should be carried to and from the car.

Equipment and Clothing

Dog training is practical and requires appropriate shoes and clothing. Flat, comfortable shoes are recommended for safety reasons and, as some training requires clients to get down on the ground, suitable and comfortable clothing is recommended.
